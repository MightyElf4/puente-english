# Teacher Guide

This guide covers everything a teacher needs to use Puente with their class — from setup to day-to-day use.

---

## Getting Started

### 1. Create a teacher account
Go to Puente and sign up with your email address. During signup, select **Teacher** as your account type. Teacher accounts have access to the dashboard and class management tools that are not visible to students.

### 2. Create a class
From your dashboard, click **New Class**. Give it a name (e.g. "Monday Evening — Beginner") and select the default level for that class. You can have multiple classes.

### 3. Invite students
Each class has a unique **class code**. Share this code with your students — they enter it when they create their account to join your class automatically. Students can belong to more than one class.

---

## Running Activities in Class

Puente has a **Classroom Mode** designed for projector use. Activate it from any activity page by clicking the projector icon in the top corner. Classroom Mode:
- Enlarges text and UI for visibility across a room
- Hides student-specific UI (progress bars, personal stats)
- Displays one question or card at a time, controlled by the teacher

You can use any activity in Classroom Mode — vocabulary flashcards work well for whole-class review, and grammar drills can be done as a group with students calling out answers.

---

## Creating Assignments

From your dashboard, go to **Assignments → New Assignment**. You can:
- Select an activity type (vocabulary, grammar, reading)
- Choose a topic or word set
- Set a due date (optional)
- Assign to one or more of your classes

Students see assigned activities highlighted on their home screen. Completion is tracked automatically.

---

## Uploading Your Own Content

Go to **My Content → Upload** to add your own vocabulary lists or exercises. You do not need to know how to code.

### Vocabulary lists
Fill in the form fields: word, Spanish translation, definition, example sentence, level, and topic. Add as many words as you like. Your list will appear as a flashcard set that you can assign to students or make available to anyone.

## Reading the Progress Dashboard

Your dashboard shows an overview of each class. For each student you can see:
- **Sessions this week** — how many times they logged in and practiced
- **Words studied** — total vocabulary cards reviewed
- **Accuracy by topic** — a breakdown of correct/incorrect by grammar topic or vocabulary set
- **Struggling areas** — topics where the student's accuracy falls below a threshold, flagged automatically

Use this information to decide what to review in class, who might need extra support, and which topics to assign next.

---

## Tips

- Encourage students to use Puente for even 10 minutes a day outside of class — consistent short sessions outperform long infrequent ones
- The resources page has free apps and tools students can use independently — point them there early
- Word of the Day is a good low-stakes conversation starter at the beginning of class
